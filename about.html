A little bit more about me...

I am an interaction designer/front-end developer who loves working with all aspects of the front-end of the web, pushing myself to keep up with the latest trends and learn new skills. I have worked on a variety of projects, from small to large, for myself and for employers, helping me gain experience as a front-end developer and an interaction designer, with an all round skillset for front-end web work, UX, and design.

The thing I'm currently most excited by in my work is SVG and CSS animations - bringing pages to life by adding subtle movements and interactions. To me, it is all about making things seem natural and seamless to the user, not having things flying across the screen from everywhere and anywhere.

...and my skills

The skills that I use day to day are HTML5, SASS/LESS/CSS3, JavaScript and jQuery. I design responsively, either custom or using frameworks such as Susy, Foundation, Bootstrap. I also use the majority of the Adobe creative suite daily, mainly Photoshop or Illustrator. I use these mainly to aid in concept work, the development of site assets. There are many other different softwares and technologies that I use in my work, such as Git, SVN, Compass, Gulp, Grunt, Node, Stash, Confluence, Axure, to name a few.

Prototyping is an important part of testing what I think is good design so I have started using things such as Framer.js and Marvelapp to try to give my prototypes a good level of fidelity. However, when possible, I prefer to hand code prototypes, ensuring working functionality of the core areas that need testing.

I have mainly worked within an agile project environment, working in scrums to deliver projects component by component, mixed with a lean UX approach to constantly test and develop work from sprint to sprint. I also have a good understanding of UX methodologies, as I have worked very closely with UX researchers and designers in my role as an Interaction designer.
